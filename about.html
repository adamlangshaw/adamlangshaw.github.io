About

Interested in how systems decide who gets care, opportunity, and attention.

I'm a Stanford student working at the intersection of medicine, health policy, and technology. I’m especially drawn to questions where quantitative analysis meets institutional design: how incentives shape access, how data can obscure or illuminate inequity, and how policy choices cascade through real lives.

My work includes research on health inequities in cystic fibrosis through the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine, writing for The Stanford Daily, and building analytical tools that make complicated public problems easier to reason about.

I grew up in Weston, Florida, in a multicultural family with roots in Jamaica and England. Outside the classroom, I care a lot about sports, music, and the craft of making ideas persuasive.

Research

Clinical equity and health outcomes

Co-authored work on immigrant patients with cystic fibrosis, contributing to a growing evidence base around access and health inequities.

Writing

Public argument as a serious discipline

Opinion and sports writing that treats clarity, structure, and judgment as part of the work itself.

Tools

Interactive models that teach

Building browser-based explainers that translate technical ideas in healthcare policy and algorithmic fairness into something people can actually explore.
