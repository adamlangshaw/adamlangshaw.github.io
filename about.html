About

Adam Langshaw

Adam Langshaw is a Stanford student working at the intersection of medicine, health policy, and technology. He studies Public Policy with a concentration in health policy and Mathematics, with a minor in Computer Science. His work focuses on how data, institutional design, and incentive structures shape healthcare delivery and outcomes.

He has co-authored research on health inequities in cystic fibrosis through the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine, published in the Journal of Cystic Fibrosis and presented at the North American Cystic Fibrosis Conference. He writes opinion and sports coverage for The Stanford Daily.

He grew up in Weston, Florida, in a multicultural family with roots in Jamaica and England. Outside of academics, he follows Formula 1, collects opinions about Porsche 911s, and referees soccer.
