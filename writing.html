Reading with purpose

Influences that sharpen the argument

These are the books and thinkers that keep the writing from becoming too neat. They push on tradeoffs, incentives, dignity, and power, which is usually where the best arguments begin.

Being Mortal

Atul Gawande

A reminder that institutions are judged by how they treat people at their most vulnerable moments.

A Theory of Justice

John Rawls

Still one of the cleanest ways to think about fairness before outcomes get messy.

The Power Broker

Robert Caro

A model for how power, infrastructure, and institutions shape one another over time.
